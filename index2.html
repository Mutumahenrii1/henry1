PIG FARMING

what is a pig

A pig is a domesticated mammal known for its stout body with four legs, a short snout, and a curly tail. Pigs are highly intelligent animals and are often raised for their meat, which is called pork. They are also raised for other products such as leather, lard, and gelatin.

types of pigs

  • Large White (Yorkshire): This is one of the most common breeds used in commercial pig farming. Large Whites are known for their prolificacy, mothering ability, and lean meat production.

  • Hampshire: Hampshire pigs are easily recognizable by their black bodies with a white belt around their shoulders. They are valued for their meat quality, carcass leanness, and efficient feed conversion.

  • Berkshire: Berkshire pigs are black with white markings on their face, legs, and tail. They are known for their marbled meat, which is highly prized by chefs and consumers for its tenderness and flavor.

  • Landrace: Landrace pigs are long-bodied with large, floppy ears. They are known for their excellent maternal instincts, large litters, and high milk production.

  • Tamworth: Tamworth pigs are ginger-colored with erect ears. They are known for their hardiness, foraging ability, and flavorful meat.
